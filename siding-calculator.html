How to calculate how much siding you need

Siding covers your exterior walls, so the job is to add up your total wall area and convert it into the units siding is sold in. The method is: measure each wall, add a waste allowance, then divide by 100 to get squares (the standard siding unit). If you know the coverage per box, this calculator also works out the boxes and total cost.

The formula, step by step

1. Wall area = height × width for each wall. For triangular gable ends, switch the row to Gable and the calculator applies ½ × base × height automatically.
2. Net area = sum of all walls and gables − openings (doors, windows, garage doors).
3. With waste = net area × (1 + waste % ÷ 100).
4. Squares = area ÷ 100. Boxes = area ÷ box coverage.

Trim and accessories — estimated for you

The squares cover the flat wall field only, so this calculator also estimates the pieces sold separately by the linear foot: starter strip (base of each wall), corner posts (from your outside-corner count), J-channel (around each door and window), and siding nails (~1.5 lbs per square). Soffit and fascia depend on roof overhang, so price those separately. For a home with many openings and gables, lean toward a 15% waste allowance — every opening and angle creates offcuts you can't reuse.

Subtracting openings

Enter your door and window counts and the calculator subtracts them at standard sizes — an entry door at 3′ × 7′ (21 sq ft) and an average window at 3′ × 5′ (15 sq ft). For oversized openings like garage doors or picture windows, add their area in the Large openings field. If you prefer the pro habit of keeping small windows as a safety margin, just leave the counts at zero.

Cost range: materials vs. installed

Installed cost is usually about double the material cost once labor is included — typically $2–$5 per square foot for installation. A 1,500 sq ft siding job in vinyl might run about $3,000–$7,500 material plus a similar amount for labor; fiber cement lands noticeably higher.

DIY vs. professional install

Installing siding yourself can save roughly half the total cost, since labor is often $2–$5 per square foot. Vinyl is the most forgiving for a confident DIYer — it's light, cuts with tin snips, and clips together. Fiber cement and metal are heavy, need special blades and dust protection, and mistakes are costly, so most homeowners hire a pro for those. Factor in scaffolding, correct flashing, and a straight starter row — a crooked start telegraphs all the way up the wall.

Siding calculator — vinyl, fiber cement, and wood siding square footage

Vinyl siding calculator

Vinyl siding is the most affordable option ($3–$8 per sq ft installed). A 2,000 sq ft home typically needs 2,000–2,500 sq ft of siding (accounting for waste, gables, and features). The calculator helps you estimate both material and labor budgets. Vinyl lasts 20–40 years and requires minimal maintenance.

Fiber cement siding calculator

Fiber cement (like Hardie board) costs $6–$12 per sq ft installed and lasts 25–50 years. It's more durable than vinyl and weathers better. A 2,000 sq ft home at 8-inch coverage needs about 2,500–3,000 sq ft of material accounting for overlaps and trim.

Wood siding calculator

Wood siding ranges from $8–$15 per sq ft installed and requires regular maintenance (repainting every 5–7 years). Shiplap and clapboard are classic choices. Square footage calculations are similar to vinyl, but waste factor is often higher due to cutting around corners and openings.

Shingle and shake siding calculator

Wood shingles and shakes are premium options ($10–$20+ per sq ft) but offer distinctive aesthetics. Shake is thicker and more rustic; shingles are finer. Both are sold in "squares" (100 sq ft per square). A 2,500 sq ft wall needs 25 squares, plus 1–2 extra for waste.

Metal siding calculator

Metal (aluminum or steel) siding is durable ($5–$10 per sq ft) and low-maintenance. It's common in commercial buildings and industrial aesthetics. Calculations are identical to vinyl, but installation costs may be higher if professional work is needed.

Brick and stone veneer siding calculator

Brick veneer costs $8–$15 per sq ft installed and adds significant curb appeal. A 2,000 sq ft home might use 4,000–5,000 bricks (accounting for waste and the fact that bricks cover about 7–8 sq ft per 100 bricks). Stone veneer is pricier but similarly calculated.

Horizontal vs. vertical siding — does width matter?

Siding width (4 to 8 inches) affects coverage: narrower boards cover less area per piece, so you need more pieces and more waste. 4-inch clapboard needs about 10% more material than 8-inch vinyl for the same wall area. The calculator factors this into estimates.

Siding and fascia — don't forget trim materials

Trim, fascia, soffit, and corner boards can add 10–20% to material needs. A 2,000 sq ft siding job often needs 200–400 linear feet of trim. Budget an extra $500–$1,500 for trim materials depending on siding type and home complexity.

How long does siding last?

Vinyl: 20–40 years. Fiber cement: 25–50 years. Wood: 20–40 years (with maintenance). Metal: 40–50+ years. Brick: 50+ years or indefinite. Choose based on your climate and how long you plan to stay in the home.

Methodology & sources

How this calculator works: wall area = height × width; gable area = ½ × base × height; openings are subtracted at standard sizes (entry door 3′ × 7′ = 21 sq ft, average window 3′ × 5′ = 15 sq ft); squares = net area with waste ÷ 100. Accessory estimates use common installer rules of thumb: starter strip along the base of each wall, one full-height corner post per outside corner, J-channel of ≈17 lin ft per door and ≈16 lin ft per window, and ≈1.5 lbs of nails per square of vinyl siding.

Sources: installation practices follow the Vinyl Siding Institute (VSI) installation manual (vinylsiding.org). Cost ranges are compiled from published price lists of major U.S. home-improvement retailers and are reviewed periodically; they are estimates, not quotes. Frequently asked questions

How much siding do I need?

Measure the area of each wall (length × height), add them up, add about 10% for waste, then divide by 100 since siding is sold by the square (100 sq ft).

What is a square of siding?

A square is 100 square feet of wall coverage — the standard unit for siding. Vinyl is often sold in boxes that cover two squares (200 sq ft).

How much does siding cost per square foot?

Materials range from about $2 to $12 per square foot: vinyl $2–$5, engineered wood $4–$7, fiber cement $5–$9, and metal or natural wood $6–$12. Installed prices are typically double.

Is it cheaper to install siding yourself?

DIY vinyl can save roughly half the total cost since labor runs $2–$5 per square foot. Vinyl is the most DIY-friendly; fiber cement and metal are usually best left to professionals.

How much extra siding should I buy?

Add about 10% for a simple rectangular house and 15% for homes with lots of corners, gables, and openings. Buy trim pieces such as J-channel and corner posts separately.
