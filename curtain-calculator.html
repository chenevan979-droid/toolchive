About This Calculator

The key to great-looking curtains is fullness — the ratio of total fabric width to window width. Flat panels at 1:1 look skimpy; 2× fullness creates a classic gathered look; 2.5–3× is used for formal or luxurious drapes.

Fullness guide

  • 1.5× (light): Minimal gather, modern/minimalist look
  • 2× (standard): Classic look, works for most rooms
  • 2.5–3× (full/luxe): Rich, formal drapes with deep folds

Hem allowance

Add 6–8 inches for a standard double-fold bottom hem and 2 inches for a top header. For floor-length drapes that puddle, add an extra 6–12 inches to your drop.

Frequently asked questions

How does this curtain calculator decide how many panels I need?

The curtain calculator multiplies your window width by the fullness ratio you pick (2× is the standard gathered look), then divides by the panel width to find how many panels give proper, even fullness.

What fullness should curtains have?

A 1.5× ratio looks light and flat, 2× is the standard gathered look, and 2.5–3× gives a luxurious full drape. Sheer fabrics usually look best at higher fullness.

How much fabric do I need to sew my own curtains?

The tool estimates yardage from your drop height plus hem allowance, multiplied by the number of panels. Add extra if your fabric has a pattern repeat that needs matching.
